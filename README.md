# Mini-Challenge 2

## Overview

Mini-Challenge 2 focuses on **conflict over societal change**. Two opposing groups — representing the **fishing** and **tourism** industries — are competing for economic development funding. Both accuse a local government oversight board of bias.

Your task is to reconstruct an accurate timeline and analyse evidence from datasets provided by both sides in order to determine whether these accusations are supported by the data.

---

## Background

For decades, **Oceanus** relied on a stable, fishing-based economy. Recently, however, tourism has expanded rapidly, reshaping the local economic landscape.

To manage this transition, the local government created an oversight board:

> **Commission on Overseeing the Economic Future of Oceanus (COOTEFOO)**

COOTEFOO:
- Is an appointed board  
- Has no direct authority over rules or spending  
- Holds significant influence within the civil service  
- Receives public funding  
- Is expected to operate in an unbiased manner  

### The Controversy

COOTEFOO is now at the centre of political tension between two groups:

---

### 1. Fishing is Living and Heritage (FILAH)

FILAH claims:
- COOTEFOO is biased toward tourism  
- The board is neglecting Oceanus’ historical economic backbone — fishing  
- Some members are prejudiced against the fishing industry  

---

### 2. Tourism Raises OceanUs Together (TROUT)

TROUT claims:
- COOTEFOO favours entrenched fishing interests  
- The board appeases the fishing industry  
- Some members are resistant to economic modernisation  

---

## The Investigation

Journalist **Edwina Darling Moray (E.D.)**, from the *Haacklee Herald*, is investigating government accountability.

She has obtained:
- The datasets used by both TROUT and FILAH  
- Meeting minutes  
- Travel records of COOTEFOO members  
- Additional records not previously available to either group  

A staff programmer has transformed the data into a **knowledge graph**. You are continuing this work by designing **visual analytics tools** to help Moray understand the situation.

Moray:
- Is not a knowledge graph expert  
- Has deep contextual knowledge of Oceanus  
- Needs intuitive visual tools to evaluate the accusations  

---

# Tasks

## 1️⃣ Analyse Each Dataset Independently

Using the datasets provided separately by TROUT and FILAH:

- Develop visualisations to detect potential bias in each dataset.  
- Determine whether each group’s accusations are supported by their own evidence.  
- Is there observable bias in COOTEFOO member actions within either dataset?  

---

## 2️⃣ Analyse the Combined Knowledge Graph

Using the full dataset (TROUT + FILAH + additional records):

- Design visual analytics to understand how COOTEFOO members allocate their time.  
- Assess whether the committee, as a whole, shows systematic bias.  
- Provide clear visual evidence supporting your conclusions.  

---

## 3️⃣ Compare Partial vs Complete Data

Since TROUT and FILAH datasets are incomplete:

- Compare conclusions drawn from each partial dataset with conclusions from the complete dataset.  
- Do TROUT’s accusations become:  
  - Strengthened?  
  - Weakened?  
  - Unchanged?  

Explain how context alters interpretation.

---

## 4️⃣ Individual-Level Behaviour Analysis

Design a visualisation that allows Moray to:

- Select an individual COOTEFOO member  
- Compare how their behaviour appears across:  
  - TROUT dataset  
  - FILAH dataset  
  - Complete dataset  

Focus on how the narrative changes depending on the data source.

### Required Analysis

- Select at least one COOTEFOO member accused by TROUT.  
- Show how your understanding of their actions changes when using the full dataset.  
- Identify key missing evidence in the original TROUT dataset that affected judgement.  
- Determine whose behaviour is most distorted by sampling bias in the FILAH dataset.  
- Illustrate the nature of FILAH’s dataset bias within the full context.  

---

## Objective

Build visual analytics tools that:

- Reveal patterns of influence and activity  
- Detect sampling bias  
- Distinguish perception from evidence  
- Help a non-technical journalist interpret complex structured data
